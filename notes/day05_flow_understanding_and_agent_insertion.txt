1. OpenROAD Baseline Flow Understanding
The baseline flow transforms RTL into final routed layout. For the gcd design, the main stages are synthesis, floorplan, placement, CTS, routing, and final report generation.

2. Where the Agent Is Inserted
The agent does not replace OpenROAD internal C++ algorithms yet. Instead, it works at the flow-control level. It reads timing/QoR reports, generates candidate constraints and configuration parameters, and reruns OpenROAD.

3. Strategy Parameters Modified by the Agent
The current prototype modifies:
- clock_period in SDC
- CORE_UTILIZATION in config.mk
- PLACE_DENSITY_LB_ADDON in config.mk
- TNS_END_PERCENT in config.mk

4. Why Placement Matters
Placement affects wirelength, congestion, and parasitic RC. The fixed-clock experiments show that simply reducing utilization does not necessarily improve timing, because more whitespace can also increase wirelength.

5. Why Constraint Feasibility Matters
The original 0.46 ns target remains difficult to close. The LLM-generated constraint-feasibility strategy relaxes the clock to 0.55 ns and achieves WNS=0, TNS=0, and zero setup violations.

6. Difference from Basic OpenROAD Tutorial
The HackMD-style OpenROAD notes explain what each flow stage does. My project goes further by adding an LLM/agent layer that automatically explores design-specific strategies and selects the best QoR result.
